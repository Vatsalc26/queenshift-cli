# Queenshift Code Of Conduct

Queenshift should feel safe for first-time contributors, careful bug reporters, and people asking basic questions.

The goal is not performative policy.

The goal is a calm public repo where people can collaborate without harassment, pile-ons, or pressure to widen claims beyond evidence.

## Expected Behavior

1. be respectful, specific, and evidence-backed
2. assume good intent and ask clarifying questions before escalating
3. critique code, docs, or behavior without attacking people
4. redact secrets and private repo material when sharing logs or artifacts
5. leave room for beginners, non-native speakers, and bounded disagreement

## Unacceptable Behavior

1. harassment, threats, hate speech, or personal attacks
2. doxxing or posting private tokens, credentials, or sensitive repo data
3. spam, repeated bad-faith derailment, or dogpiling
4. pressuring maintainers to bypass documented safety boundaries or widen claims without proof

## Reporting

1. use the repository host's moderation or reporting tools when they are available
2. use `SECURITY.md` instead of public threads for security-sensitive reports
3. the maintainer may edit, hide, lock, or remove content that breaks this code of conduct

## Scope

This applies to issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions, and other public collaboration spaces operated for Queenshift.
